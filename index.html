Hi, I’m Shreyam

Final-year undergraduate at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) BHU, Varanasi, majoring in Mechanical Engineering. My research lies at the intersection of deep learning, computer vision, and safety-critical autonomous systems and navigation.

Under the guidance of Prof. Jahar Sarkar and Prof. Indra D Mastan at Pattern Recognition Lab, IIT BHU, I'm currently developing a novel framework that integrates physically guided attention-based neural networks for real-world problems such as early wildfire detection.

I have also contributed as a Research Intern at the University of Colorado Boulder, developing the Multi-Attention Unit (MAUCell), a novel recurrent neural architecture that intelligently fuse spatial and temporal features for video frame prediction. And Drone-Assisted UAV aided mapping and localization for a UGV to autonomously traverse in mountainous terrains.

Beyond research, I have led technical teams as the Technical Lead of RoBoReg. And served as a coordinator for Team AVERERA, led the development of a semantic segmentation model deployed to an autonomous vehicle for real-world testing.

Outside the lab, you’ll often find me strumming my guitar or getting lost in a canvas—keeps my ideas fresh and my research inspired.

MAUCell: Adaptive Multi-Attention Framework for Video Frame Prediction

A new RNN-based spatio-temporal prediction unit with attention termed Multi-Attention Unit (MAUCell) that combines temporal motion information and spatial appearance information respectively through a temporal attention unit and a spatial attention unit to preserve long-term sequence information at high resolution. The outputs from the two attention units are next aggregated through a hybrid aggregation unit with a wide receptive field for both the features, which causes high quality video prediction. The above units are embedded within a GAN framework that is trained in an end-to-end fashion.

Time-Series Thermal Translation Using Physically-Guided Spatio-Temporal Attention

Pattern Recognition Lab - IIT BHU

PT-STAFU is a novel deep learning framework designed for accurate translation of time-series thermal video data between different sensor modalities. By integrating physics-based thermal modeling with advanced spatio-temporal attention mechanisms, within a GAN-based architecture, the project enables consistent, physically plausible synthesis of thermal video sequences across domains.

Particularly suited for wildfire prevention, where early detection and prediction of thermal anomalies in multi-sensor video streams are critical for timely intervention and risk mitigation.
